## Description

史溫侯描述福爾摩沙的爬蟲類和蝙蝠類動物，包含革龜（Dermochelys coriacea （L.））、斯文豪氏攀蜥（Iapalura swinhoii, Günth. l. c.，目前學名 Diploderma swinhonis）、龜殼花（原文Trimeresurus mucrosquamatus （Cantor）; Günth. l. c.，目前學名Protobothrops mucrosquamatus）等18種爬蟲類。

## 中文 (TRANSLATION)

##--SPLIT--##
採集自中國各地區之爬蟲類和兩棲類之記事。作者羅伯特．史溫侯。
（圖31.）

    1. 平胸龜（Platysternum megacephalum, Gray，目前學名Platysternon megacephalum）；貢德（Günth.）。《英屬印度的爬蟲類（Reptiles of Brit. Ind）》，第43頁。
     我在廣州（Canton）的一家鳥店裡買到了一隻有趣的烏龜標本。牠是來自於沿著廣東省（Kwangtung Province）西部和廣西省（Kwangse Province）順流而下的西江（West River）。根據紀錄，牠的頭部、四肢和尾巴無法縮回龜殼內；尾巴捲起後可收在龜甲下緣保護。就算都沒給食物，我的平胸龜還是活過了整個冬天。
    2. 革龜（Dermochelys coriacea （L.））
    在福爾摩沙島周圍的溫暖海域中，經常有綠蠵龜（Green Turtle）和革龜出現，但是這些海龜很少出現

##--SPLIT--##
在中國沿岸水溫較低的海域中。我待在廈門（Amoy）的那幾年，從來沒聽說過有任何海龜被捕獲，除了1859年10月的那一次。那是一個體型和年紀都相當大的個體，呈現帶淺黃的茜紅色。我原本想買下來收藏，但是無法說服漁民將牠賣給我。他們說，除非有大災難來臨，否則海龜從來不會出現在中國沿海，要避開這種狀況，善良的人必須將其從捕獲者手中救出來並放生。鎮上有間中國商號自願做這件善舉。他們買下海龜，並且在龜甲上刻上中文字，意思是「永遠自由」。人們在銘文上填入朱紅色顏料，並用緞帶裝飾海龜後，用小船載著牠，一行人浩浩蕩蕩來到港口的外圍，再將海龜放回海裡。他們說，如果這隻海龜再被抓到，背面的銘文可以保護牠免於遭受再一次的粗暴對待。
烏龜對中國人來說，是長壽的象徵。3. 某種鱷屬物種（Crocodilus, sp.，目前學名 Crocodylus）？
1869年2月，一群中國人在上海縣城（native city of Shanghai）展出他們稱之為「龍」的生物。他們宣稱這隻「龍」是從陝西省的（province of Shense）洞裡挖出來的。那是一隻體長約4英呎的年幼鱷魚，他們將牠放在溫水中。展示這隻鱷魚讓他們賺了很多錢，所以他們不願意把牠賣給我。我當然不能隨便猜測牠是哪一種鱷魚，但我仍然認為這是一件值得記錄的事，可以證明這類生物中，確實有一個物種存在於中國。4. 北草蜥（Tachydromus septentrionalis, Günth. l. c.1，目前學名 Takydromus septentrionalis），第70頁。
有個標本是從南京（Nanking）城牆的牆頭上抓到的。大英博物館則有來自寧波（Ningpo）的數個標本。牠是地棲型的蜥蜴，會躲藏在石頭底下。5. 麗斑麻蜥（Eremias argus （Peters））
麗斑麻蜥在北京（Peking）的農地中非常普遍。大英博物館有我在1860年取得的標本。但是當皇家學會知道這些是我在華北戰役（North-China campaign）2期間收集的標本時，便刻意不提這些標本（請參閱 P. Z. S. 1861，第391頁）。6. 中國石龍子（Mabouia chinensis （Gray），目前學名 Plestiodon chinensis），第83頁。
標本採集自漁翁島（Pescadores）。在漁翁群島最大島上的小米田裡非常普遍。屬於小族群，身上有許多黑點。7. 寧波滑蜥（Eumeces modestus, Günth. l. c.，目前學名 Scincella modesta），第87頁。

這種背部梳狀的樹棲型蜥蜴，以前只知分布於福爾摩沙島南部的森林。在我有一次沿著揚子江往上游探險時，才在重慶府附近樹林中的岩石上發現了牠。因此，牠在中國的分布範圍勢必相當廣泛。10. 臺灣黑眉錦蛇（Elaphis taeniurus, Cope; Günth. l. c.，目前學名 Orthriophis taeniurus），第242頁。

貢德（Günther）博士說，這是分布最廣的印度水棲型蛇類，分布範圍從美索不達米亞（Mesopotamia）平原一直延伸到中國南部。我的標本來自廈門。12. 草腹鏈蛇（Tropidonotus stolatus （Linn.）, Günth. l. c. ，目前學名 Amphiesma stolatum），第266頁。
標本採集自廈門。這是目前為止在廈門和淡水（福爾摩沙島）最常見的水蛇。在淡水，鄉下小伙子們一次就給我帶了好幾十條這種水蛇。13. 鉛色水蛇（Hypsirhina plumbea （Boie）, Günth. l. c.，目前學名 Enhydris plumbea），第280頁。
標本採集自廈門，此物種在那裏並不普遍。14. 彩虹水蛇（Hypsirhina enhydris, Russell;Günth. l. c.，目前學名 Enhydris enhydris），第281頁。
標本也是採集自廈門。15. 中國水蛇（Hypsirhina chinensis, Gray; Günth. l. c.，目前學名 Myrrophis chinensis），第283頁。
此物種是在長江上游1,111英里的宜昌（Ichang）捕獲的。大英博物館有收藏來自中國的標本，但是並不清楚是來自中國何處。16. 青環海蛇（Hydrophis cyanocincta, Russell; Günth. l. c.，目前學名 Hydrophis cyanocinctus），第367頁。
這種常見的海蛇常常漂流到臺灣西南部小型港口──打狗（Takow3）。我的標本是從那裡取得的。17. 龜殼花（原文Trimeresurus mucrosquamatus （Cantor）; Günth. l. c.，目前學名Protobothrops mucrosquamatus），第390頁，（圖 31.）

貢德博士好心地提供給我關於這種蛇的具體特徵：
鱗片為25列，尖端具有很堅固的鱗脊，有時候會些微突出。腹鱗則有217片至200片。頭頂上有很小的顆粒狀鱗片；沿著吻棱有一系列較大的鱗片。上唇有11枚，第二枚在前壁有凹陷。 軀幹為褐色，背面有一系列接近菱形、有時會連在一起的斑點。沿著側面下腹有另一組相似且更圓的斑點。每個斑點均為深褐色，邊緣為黑色，通常有細窄的白邊。其腹部帶有褐色紋路，每個腹側盾的側面都有卵形的白色斑點。18. 赤尾青竹絲（Trimeresurus erythrurus （Cantor）, Günth. l. c.，目前學名Trimeresurus stejnegeri），第386頁。
採集自福爾摩沙島的打狗。這是我第一次在在福爾摩沙島發現這種小型綠色蝮蛇。我很常在廈門看到牠。牠偏好在灌木中活動，爬到植物的頂端，盤繞在莖葉上。其身體呈鮮綠色，因此不容易被發現。在捕捉昆蟲的時候，我的手險些就要碰到這有毒的生物，中國人說被牠咬傷會致命。19. 日本蝮（Halys blomhoffii （Boie）, Günth. l. c.，目前學名Gloydius blomhoffii），第393頁。
從揚子江的宜昌上岸的幾位船員，將這種帶有斑點的棕色蝮蛇帶到砲艦上。已知這種物種在日本和福爾摩沙島皆有分布，但這是第一次在中國本土發現牠的存在。20. 澤蛙（Rana gracilis, Wiegm.; Günth. l. c.，目前學名Fejervarya limnocharis），第409頁。
採集自宜昌。水稻田裡很普遍的蛙類。21. 林蛙（Rana silvatica，Leconte; Günth. l. c. ），第409頁。
採集自宜昌。可在樹林中，枯葉和腐爛的草堆中發現。22. 花姬蛙（Diplopelma pulchrum （Hallon）; Günth. l. c.，目前學名Microhyla pulchra），第417頁。
採集自宜昌。我在中國和福爾摩沙島的各處都看過這種小型蛙類。5在春季和仲夏的降雨期間，會有成千上萬隻花姬蛙出現在草地上。牠不太會出現在水稻田裡。23. 長趾纖蛙（Hylorana macrodactyla,Günth. l. c.，目前學名Hylarana macrodactyla），第424頁。
採集自宜昌。牠不會爬樹，大部分的時間都待在樹林周圍潮濕的地面上。

## 英文 (ORIGINAL)

##--SPLIT--## 5. Note on Reptiles and Batrachians collected in various parts of China. By R. SWINHOE.
(Plate XXXI.) 1. PLATYSTERNUM MEGACEPHALUM, Gray ; Günth. Reptiles of Brit. Ind. p. 43.
I procured a single specimen of this curious tortoise in a bird-shop at Canton. It is brought down the West River from the western parts of Kwangtung Province and from Kwangse. As recorded, the head, limbs, and tail are not capable of retraction within the shell ; the tail is protected by curling under the right margin of the carapace. I kept my specimen alive through the winter without food. 2. DERMATOCHELYS CORIACEA (L.).
In the warm waters round Formosa the Green and the Tortoise- shell Turtles frequently occur ; but these seldom show themselves in

##--SPLIT--##
the colder seas of the China coast. During the years I spent at Amoy I never heard of a Turtle being captured but once ; this was in October 1859. It was a large old specimen of this species, of a yellowish madder-colour. I wanted to buy it to preserve ; but nothing would induce the fishermen to sell it for this purpose. They said a Turtle never appears on the China coast except when some great calamity is imminent, and to avert this good men must deliver it from its captors and set it free. A Chinese firm in the town volunteered to do the good act. They purchased the Turtle, had Chinese characters cut on its back, signifying "set free for ever," which were filled in with vermilion, and, decking it with ribbons, took it in a boat in great state, with drums beating, to the outer limits of the harbour, where it was dropped into the sea. They said that if caught again the inscription on its back would protect it from meet- ing with further rough treatment.
The Turtle is with the Chinese an emblem of longevity. 3. CROCODILUS, sp. ?
In February 1869 some Chinese were exhibiting in the native city of Shanghai what they called a Dragon, which they declared had been dug out of a hole in the province of Shense. It was a young Crocodile about 4 feet long, which they kept in tepid water. They made so much money by showing it that they refused to sell it. I cannot, of course, guess its species ; but I nevertheless think the fact worth recording, as evidence that a species of this group does occur in China. 4. TACHYDROMUS SEPTENTRIONALIS, Günth. l. c. p. 70.
One specimen caught on the top of the city wall of Nanking. The Museum has specimens from Ningpo. It is a ground-Lizard, hiding under stones. 5. EREMIAS ARGUS (Peters).
Very common in the cultivated fields about Peking. The Museum has specimens procured by me in 1860; but mention of these was omitted when the Society was made acquainted with the specimens collected by me during the North-China campaign (see P. Z. S. 1861, p. 391). 6. MABOUIA CHINENSIS (Gray), Günth. l. c. p. 83.
Specimens from the Pescadores. Very common about the millet-fields of the largest Pescadore Island. A small race, and much spotted with black. 7. EUMECES MODESTUS, Günth. l. c. p. 87.
Found on the rocky bank of the Yangtsze river in a gorge in eastern Szechuen, 1300 miles from the sea. Before known only from Ningpo.

##--SPLIT--## 8. GECKO JAPONICUS (Dum. & Bibr.), Günth. l. c. p. 10.3.
From Chungkingfoo on the Yangtsze, in eastern Szechuen, 1459 miles from the sea. Found inside houses in the city, clinging to the walls, also on the trees in gardens. Delights in uttering its “ chuck- chuck” note, especially in rainy weather. It is abundant in Taiwan- foo, Formosa. 9. IAPALURA SWINHOII, Günth. l. c. p. 133.
This comb-backed Tree-lizard was before only known from the woods of South Formosa. On my late expedition up the Yangtsze I found it on the rocks among woods near Chungkingfoo. It must therefore have a wide range across China. 10. ELAPHIS TAENIURUS, Cope ; Günth. l. c. p. 242.
Captured at Shanghai. Has been procured before at Ningpo, in the Chikiang Province. 11. TROPIDONOTUS QUINCUNCIATUS, Russell; Günth. l. c. p. 260.
Dr. Günther says that this is the most widely distributed species of the Indian Water-snakes, extending from Mesopotamia to the south of China. My specimens are from Amoy. 12. TROPIDONOTUS STOLATUS (Linn.), Günth. l. c. p. 266.
From Amoy. This is by far the commonest Water-snake in Amoy and in Tamsuy (Formosa). In the latter place the country lads brought it to me by scores at a time. 13. HYPSIRHINA PLUMBEA (Boie), Günth. l. c. p. 280.
From Amoy, where it is by no means common. 14. HYPSIRHINA ENHYDRIS, Russell; Günth. l. c. p. 281.
Also procured at Amoy. 15. HYPSIRHINA CHINENSIS, Gray ; Günth. l. c. p. 283.
Captured at Ichang, 1111 miles up the Yangtsze river. The British Museum has specimens from China ; but it is not known from what part of China. 16. HYDROPHIS CYANOCINCTA, Russell; Günth. l. c. p. 367.
This common Sea-snake is often floated into the small harbour of Takow, S.W. Formosa. My specimens are from there. 17. TRIMERESURUS MUCROSQUAMATUS (Cantor) : Günth. l. c. p. 390. (Plate XXXI.)
From Formosa. Dr. Günther has made this out to be the species described by Cantor as Trigonoeephalus miicrosquamatus (P. Z. S. 1839, p. 32), from Assam, from comparison of it with Cantor's drawing, the original specimen having been lost and no other since procured. Its occurrence in Formosa is somewhat singular. The two fine large specimens I have brought home were the only indi-

##--SPLIT--##
viduals I came across. One was brought to me from the interior; the other was captured at Takow, in the hall of a neighbouring Mandarin's office, having descended from the roof, in which it was lodged, by a rope on to a hanging lamp in pursuit of geckos (Gecko japonicus). It was tinted all over with blood-colour.
The specific characters of this snake, as kindly furnished to me by Dr. Günther, are : —
Scales in 25 rows, with a very strong keel to the tip, where it sometimes slightly projects. Ventral plates 217-220. Upperside of the head with very small granular scales ; a series of larger ones along the canthus rostralis. Eleven upper labials, the second forming the anterior wall of the pit. Body brown, with a dorsal series of subrhombic, sometimes confluent spots ; another series of similar more rounded spots along the lower part of the side. Each spot is of a dark brown colour, with a black margin, and generally a narrow white edge. Abdomen marked with brownish, each ventral shield having an ovate whitish spot on each side. 18. TRIMERESURUS ERYTHRURUS (Cantor), Günth. l. c. p. 386.
From Takow, Formosa. This is the first occurrence of this little green viper in Formosa. I have seen it frequently at Amoy ; it is a bush-loving species, climbing to the top of plants, and coiling itself round the stems and leaves. Being of a fresh-green colour, it is not easy to detect ; and while catching insects I have very narrowly escaped brushing my hand over the poisonous creature. The Chinese declare its bite to be fatal. 19. HALYS BLOMHOFFII (Boie), Günth. l. c. p. 393.
A specimen of this spotted brown Viper was brought on board the gunboat by some of the crew who had been on shore at Ichang, on the Yangtsze. It is known from Japan and Formosa ; but this is the first instance of its occurrence in China proper. 20. RANA GRACILIS, Wiegm. ; Günth. l. c. p. 409.
From Ichang. A common Frog in the rice-fields. 21. RANA SILVATICA, Leconte; Günth. l. c. p. 409.
From Ichang, Found in woods, among the dead leaves and decaying herbage. 22. DIPLOPELMA PULCHRUM (Hallon) ; Günth. l. c. p. 417.
From Ichang. I have met this wee Frog in various parts of China and Formosa. During the rains of spring and midsummer it occurs in thousands about grassy places. It does not resort much to wet rice-fields. 23. HYLORANA MACRODACATYLA, Günth. l. c. p. 424.
From Ichang. Does not climb trees, but keeps much to the damp ground about woods.
