## Description

## 英文 (ORIGINAL)

##--SPLIT--##
Formosa Camphor.
The manufacture of this article has for some years been monopolized by the Taotai (or Head Mandarin) of the island, and its sale farmed out to wealthy natives. In former years a good deal of the drug was clandestinely produced, and smuggled across to China, where it was largely bought up by foreign speculators and carried to Hong-Kong for shipment to Calcutta, at which place it finds the readiest market, being used by the natives of Hindostan for lubricating the body and other domestic purposes. But now its monopoly is so closely watched that almost the entire trade in it falls to the lucky individual whose Chinese agents can secure the monopoly. This bad system has occasioned the price of the article in Hong-Kong to increase considerably in value, and to make the profits accruing to the fortunate monopolist almost fabulous.
The cost of the drug, I learn, amounts to only six dollars at its place of manufacture. The monopolist buys it from the Mandarin at sixteen dollars the pecul and sells it in Hong-Kong at twenty-eight dollars. The gigantic laurel (laurus camphora) that yields the camphor, covers the whole line of high mountains extending north and south throughout Formosa. But as the greater part of this range is in the hands of the aborigines, the Chinese are able to gain access only to those parts of the mountains contiguous to their own territories that are possessed by the more docile tribes. The trees, as they are required, are selected for the abundance of their sap, as many are too dry to repay the labor and trouble of the undertaking.
A present is then made to the chief of the tribe to gain permission to cut down the selected trees. The best part of the tree is secured for timber, and the refuse cut up into chips. The chips are boiled in iron pots, one inverted on another, and the sublimated vapor is the desired result. The camphor is then conveyed down in carts of rude construction, and stowed in large vats, with escape holes at the bottom, whence exudes an oil, known as camphor oil, used by Chinese practitioners for its medicinal properties in rheumatic diseases. Samples of this oil have been sent home, and it may eventually become a desideratum in Europe. From the vats the camphor is stowed in bags to contain about a pecul each, and is thus exported. The Chinese Government has empowered the Formosan authorities to claim on its account all the timber produced by the island for ship-building purposes; and it is on this plea that the Taotai appropriates the prescriptive right of dealing in camphor. About 6,000 peculs of the drug are annually produced in the neighbourhood of Tamsuy—Robert Swinhoe.

## 中文 (TRANSLATION)

##--SPLIT--##
福爾摩沙的樟腦
福爾摩沙島上樟腦的生產，長久以來被島上的道台（或最高級清官1）壟斷，並將銷售發包給當地的富豪。過去幾年，大量的樟腦被秘密生產出來，然後走私到中國。在那裡，大部分是被外國投機客買下，運到香港後，再透過海運送到加爾各答（Calcutta），加爾各答成為樟腦銷路最好的地方。印度斯坦（Hindostan）的當地人則主要用樟腦來潤滑皮膚，以及用於其他日常用途。但現在樟腦的壟斷受到非常密切的注意，導致幾乎整個市場都被掌握在那個幸運兒手裡，因為他的中國代理人們能夠確保他的壟斷地位。這樣糟糕的制度導致樟腦在香港的價格大幅上漲，那個好運氣的壟斷者得到的利潤簡直高得不可思議。據我瞭解，樟腦在原產地的生產成本只有6美元。壟斷者以16美元的價格從中國官員手中購買，然後以28美元的價格在香港出售。可以提煉出樟腦的巨大樟樹（Laurus camphora，目前學名 Cinnamomum camphora）遍佈福爾摩沙島上南北綿延的山脈。但是，由於這片山脈大部分都掌握在原住民（aborigines）手中，因此中國人只能進到那些與中國領土鄰接，由較溫順的部落所佔領的山區。提煉樟腦的時候，人們會挑選富含汁液的樹木，因為許多樟樹汁液過少，產出的樟腦量與提煉過程中所花費的勞力和心力成本不成比例。
接著，他們會送一份禮物給部落的首領，以獲得砍伐樟樹的許可。他們會將樟樹最好的部位留下來做成木材，剩下的部分則被切成木片。木片會被放在鐵鍋中煮沸，再將另一只鐵鍋倒置其上，昇華後的蒸氣就是人們想得到的成果，也就是樟腦。樟腦會用簡陋的推車運送下山，裝進大桶裡，大桶底部有氣孔，從氣孔中滲出的油就是人們口中的樟腦油，中醫會用這種油來治療風濕病。樟腦油的樣品已送回英國，這種油最後可能在歐洲成為搶手貨。大桶中的樟腦會被分裝在袋子裡，每袋大約裝一擔2，接著就可以出口到國外。中國政府已授權給福爾摩沙當局島上所有用於造船之木材的所有權，而道台正是以此為藉口，獨佔樟腦交易的法定權利。淡水（Tamsuy）周邊地區每年約生產6,000擔的樟腦。──羅伯特．史溫侯。

---

1 譯註：Head Mandarin，馬來文menteri，源自梵文 mantrin，意指朝臣、部長。十六世紀初，在滿剌加國（今馬六甲）的葡萄牙人想晉見出使當地的明國官員，遂借用馬來語 menteri 指稱中國官員。因葡人不諳馬來語，便訛為 Mandarim。
2 譯註：重量單位，約30公斤。
