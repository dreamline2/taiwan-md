ARTICLE VIII

NOTES ON SOME NEW SPECIES OF BIRDS FOUND
ON THE ISLAND OF FORMOSA

By R. SWINHOE ESQ.

(Supplementary to Article I. page 145.)

ORDER II.—PASSERES. TRIBE III.—DENTIROSTRES’ FAMILY I.—LUSCINIDÆ. SUBFAMILY I.—MALURINÆ.

Two species of birds in this subfamily, not coming under any genus described by G. R. Gray, in his Genera of Birds, for which I propose a new genus under the term Calamanthella, may be thus defined:—Bill shorter than the bead, broad at the hose, but compressed towards the tip; culmen gradually arched towards the tip; goays long and ascending; nostrils placed longitudinally in a basal membranous groove; gape furnished with short weak bristles. Wings moderate; the first quill abruptly short, the second shorter than the third, which with the fourth and fifth are longest in the wing. Tail moderate, or short, and graduated. Tarsi much longer than the middle toe, and covered with in-distinct scales; toes long with the outer toe shorter than the inner, and united at the base to the middle one; the hind toe long and armed with a long curved claw.
These birds differ from the genera Prinia (Horsf.) and Calamanthus (Gould), to which they are nearly allied, by their active habits, being constantly on the wing, flying high in the air uttering; their singular notes. The two distinct species which I procured in Formosa, I have named Tinnabulans and Volitans respectively. The former of these is very common in the plains and on the hills of the N.W. side of Formosa, and I have since found it very numerous among the rush-covered tumuli in Shanghai. The latter was abundant in the E. and N. E. of Formosa. I will now venture to give a few words on the general description of the two species, as also on their habits as observed by myself.
Calamanthella tinnabulans. Bill along culmen 1/3 inch; bill to gape 7 twelfths; tarsus 2/3; onyx 1/2; middle toe 7 twelfths; wing 1 & 11 twelfths; tail 1 & 7 twelfths. Total length 4 1/2 inches.

##--SPLIT--##
Bill yellowish, wood-brown towards the tip and along the culmen. Legs ochreous yellow. Irides orange yellow. Upper parts yellowish brown varied with sienna red; the feathers on the head, back, wing-coverts, secondaries, and tail-coverts, dark-brown with yellowish brown margins. The wing-feathers hair-brown broadly margined with pale yellowish brown. Tail consisting of 12 feathers almost equally graduated; the uropygials brown-margined paler; the other 10 feathers marked with dark brown and reddish sienna, each feather being deeply tipped with white. Eye-streak, throat and under parts white tinged with sienna or straw yellow, deeper on the sides, and approaching red on the flanks.
Habits. These little sprightly birds abound in Hongsan and the N.W. coast of Formosa, among the long grass and rushes, hopping with upright tails from one blade to another, so light, so airy in their build, that one often mistakes them for locusts, with which insects their haunts abound. Suddenly springing into the air, they rise to a little height uttering the notes te-te-te, then drop with a ting-ting-ting; and when many are singing over a plain, the effect may well be compared to the “drowsy tinkling” that “lulls the distant fold.”
In Shanghai I noted,—“The Formosan Tinkler is also here, and continues to sing as he flies along in the air his singular notes; now flying downwards with a curve as if he would settle, then sweeping up again and hovering, all the while uttering his notes. At last he makes up his mind to descend, and down he comes and perches on some stalk of maize or other plant, and carefully preens, and rests himself; a few minutes more and he is again on the wing.”
The Chinese settlers in Formosa call this bird the Manytang; the same name that in Amoy is applied to the Tailor-bird (Orthotomos sp.) and most small birds of that family.
Calamanthella volitans. Bill along culmen, over 1/3 inch; bill to gape 1/2; tarsus 2/3; onyx 5 twelfths; middle toe 1/3; wing 1 2/3; tail 11 twelfths. Total length 3 1/4 inches.
Bill pale wood-brown. Legs straw color. Irides orange yellow. Head, nape and all the under parts pale sienna yellow, more or less ochreous. Back and wings hair brown, the feathers margined with light yellowish brown. Rump light sienna brown. Tail dark hair-brown; the feathers graduated, but broad at their tips giving the tail the appearance of an isosceles triangle.

##--SPLIT--##
Habits. This species, which is even smaller than the foregoing, is found in numbers among the grassy hills on the N.E. side of Formosa. It soars much higher than its congener, and flies long distances uttering notes similar to “chee-chee-cheup-cheup;” and often when the bird itself is not visible, lost in the expanse above, you are made aware that it is somewhere overhead, by the fall of its notes on your ear.

GENUS,--PRINIA. (Horsf.)
Prinia striata. (n. sp.) Bill along culmen 4 tenths inch: bill to gape 6 tenths; tarsus 9 tenths; wing 2 1/3; tail 4 & 7 tenths. Total length 8 inches.
Bill black. Legs ochreous yellow. Irides orange brown. Upper parts a rich brown, each feather having darker centres, and whitish aide-margins, giving a striped appearance. Wings yellowish hair-brown; the feathers broadly margined with yellowish brown. Tail light brown; the shafts dark, and the tips yellowish. Under-parts of a dingy yellowish tinge spotted on the cheeks and head, and striped on the sides of the neck Thighs reddish brown.
This bird was procured on the N.W. side at Hongsan, some little distance inland. It is much larger than any Prinia I have yet met with in China. In its accustomed haunts, it may be seen mounted on the top of some tall rush-stalk, singing a monotonous ditty of a few notes, somewhat resembling those of the Bunting Lark, often repeated.

FAMILY,--TURDIDE. SUBFAMILY,--FORMICARINÆ. GENUS.—HYDROBATA (Vieill) OR DIPPER.
There appear to be several Dippers, more or less similar, from totally different parts of the world. Such are the H. unicolor (Pr. Buon.) from America, the H. Pallisir (Termm.) from Europe, and the H. Asiatica (Swains). To the last species I should be inclined to assign the present bird, but there being some little doubt about it, I propose describing it under the term Hydrobata marila:—Length in total 8 1/2 inches; wing from flexure 3 3/4.
Bill and legs indigo blue; the latter thick and rough-soled, with strong claws. Irides deep brown. The whole of the body umber-brown, blacker on the under parts, and tinged with raw sienna on the upper. A line of white nearly encircles the eye, and a few indistinct whitish spots occur on the wing-coverts.

##--SPLIT--##
The specimen from which the above description is taken was a female, shot over one of the waterfalls on our inland expedition to the sulphur mines. She was seen to take one plunge, and was sitting with tail erect on a rock in the gliding stream.

SUBFAMILY III.--TIMALINÆ, OR BABBLERS. GENUS,--GARRULAX (Leal).
Garrulax Thereanus. (n. sp.) The Song-thrush or Hwa-mei (G. Sinensis), remarkable for a white eye-streak, so common on the bills in the vicinity of Foochow, is replaced on the Formosan hills by a dingier species, which is destitute of the eyebrowed distinction; and may be thus described:—Male: length 9 3/4 inches; wing 3 3/4; tail 4 1/4. Female: length 9 inches; wing 3 & 7 twelfths; tail 3 3/4. Bill of both along culmen 8 tenths; bill to gape 9 tenths; tarsus 1 & 9 tenths.
Bill horn-yellow. Legs ochreous. Irides grey. Top of the head and under parts buff ochre; the feathers of the head, and some few on the sides of the neck and breast, being streaked with black. Belly smoke grey. Upper parts dingy olive brown, brighter on the wings, and transversely barred on the tail with indistinct bars.
The song of this species is very fine, but, like all this genus of babbling birds, it is very rarely that it will mug a set song of many minutes duration. While rambling over the hills, I have been startled by a burst of delicious melody from some neighboring bush, and on approaching nearer, have spied this bird flit noiselessly into an adjoining cover, where with several of his comrades he would keep up a constant chatter until I removed some distance off. The male and female are very much similar in dress.

GENUS,--POMATORRHINUS (Horsf).
This has been noted as a genus of unmusical Thrushes, whereas the species abounding in Formosa is remarkable for its sweet song, and very different from the small Wren-like species procured on the Pihling hills near Foochow.
Pomatorrhinus musicus n. sp. Male: length 7 & 4 tenths inches. Female: length 7 & 8 tenths inches. Wing 3 1/3 inches; tail 3 1/2; bill along culmen 8 tenths; bill to gape 1; tarsus 1 1/3; onyx 9 tenths; middle toe 1 & 1 tenth.
Bill, black on the upper mandible, yellowish on the lower. Legs deep indigo blue. Top of the head and cheeks blackish green.

##--SPLIT--##
Space between the bill and eyes deep black. A streak over the eyes, throat and breast white, the latter blotched with black. Nape, back of the neck, and upper part of the belly, fine chesnut red. The rest of the plumage olive green; in the males deeply tinged with chesnut, especially on the wings.
These Thrushes enliven the copse-covered hills with their song. They may be seen about the branches of some tree that lifts itself higher than the surrounding brushwood, hopping from twig to twig with rounded backs. On alarm they drop with amazing velocity into the scrub beneath, and there chatter and gabble in an under-tone, as if telling one another what they had seen and what a narrow escape they had just had. From these retreats the male loves to pour its melody into the delighted ear of the passerby, and the nearer the approach the louder sound the notes, until the bird thinking the propinquity of the stranger too great an intrusion, quietly slips out at the back of the bush, and betakes himself to some other spot, and while you are waiting anxiously to get a look at him, you hear him exerting his musical powers some distance off.

ORDER III.—SCANSORRS. FAMILY IV.—CUCULIDÆ. SUBFAMILY III.—COCCYZINÆ. (Ground Cuckoos.) GENUS,—CENTROPUS (Illig.)

Centropus dimidiatus, (Blyth?) Length 12 3/4 inches; wing 5 & 9 tenths; tail 7; hill along culmen 1; bill to gape 1 & 1 tenth; tarsus 1 & 4 tenths.
The feathers of the head, neck, and wing-coverts have stiff acuminated shafts. Bill and legs black. Irides blood red. Head, neck, under parts, and tail, greenish black. Back and wings light chesnut-brown; the shafts of most of the feathers, especially on the wing-coverts, being yellowish white. The quills browner towards their tips. Feathers of the rump very soft, and transversely barred with brown and dingy yellow.
On first meeting with this bird on the N.E. coast of Formosa among the hills of Saw-o and Kelung, I took it to be the same species that is so abundant in the valley of Foochow, (Centropus Philippensis Cuv. or Crow Pheasant of the foreign residents), remarkable for its notes “hoo-hoo-hoo,” which have been compared to the noise produced by the stops of an organ suddenly drawn out. But on procuring a male bird, from which the above description is

##--SPLIT--##
taken, I found it to be scarcely more than one half the size of the other species and differing from it also in color. Besides the notes are different, the “hoo-hoo-hoo” being followed by the dissyllable “ka-took” thrice repeated, the sounds produced somewhat resembling the distant blows of a woodman’s axe, as it falls on the trunk of some devoted patriarch of the forest. On looking up the face of a hill you might often see this small Larkheel sitting conspicuous on the top of some bush and bending forward its head and neck as it gave utterance to its strange notes.
To the above I may also add another bird, which, though not a new species, I have not as yet found on the coast of China. Such is the Philippine Noddy.

ORDER VIII.—ANSERES. FAMILY V.—LARIDÆ. SUBFAMILY III.—STERNIDÆ (Terns). GENUS,―ANÖUS (Leach).
Anöus pileatus. (Scop.) Philippine Noddy. Male: length 14 1/2 inches; wing 10 & 8 tenths; tail 6 1/5. Female: length 14 1/2 inches; wing 10 & 8 tenths; tail 6 & 3 tenths; bill along the culmen 1 1/2; bill to gape 2 1/5; tibia bare about 1/2 inch above the knee; tarsus 9 tenths; middle toe 1 & 6 tenths; onyx 4 tenths.
Bill and legs black.. Iris dark brown. Forehead and top of the head white, gradually changing as it passes to the occiput into smoke grey, which again darkens into brown. Space between the bill and eyes, and chin, dark black. A circle of white round the eyes. The rest of the plumage brownish-black, of a deeper color on the quills.
A male of this species flew so close past the ship’s gig in Saw-o harbor that a seaman knocked it down with his hand, and at Kelung a female was brought alive to us by some fishermen for sale. Far away from land I have often watched these Black Terns following at some distance in the wake of the ship, darting backwards and forwards in numbers across the broad trail left in the water by the steamer speeding onward on her course, and I suppose preying upon the numerous small fish that, startled at the agitated waters, commit themselves for awhile to the treacherous air, distrusting the state of their natural element.

文章八
福爾摩沙島尋獲之新鳥種紀事
作者史溫侯先生
（補充文章一，頁145。）
第二目：燕雀目（PASSERRS）。第三群：DENTIROSTRES。第一科：LUSCINIDAE。第一亞科：細尾鷯鶯亞科（MALURINAE）。
這個亞科底下的兩種鳥無法歸類在喬治・羅伯特・格雷（G. R. Gray）《鳥類大全》（Genera of Birds）書中所描述的任何一屬，因此我建議為牠創建一個新的屬名「Calamanthella」，[1]定義如下：喙比頭部短，基部寬大，末端呈扁長形；上喙嘴峰逐漸拱起；下喙嘴底長而向上傾斜；鼻孔呈縱向，位於底部的膜狀溝中；嘴裂（gape）長有短而軟的剛毛。翅膀長度一般；第一根飛羽特別短，第二根又比第三根更短，第四和第五根則是翅膀上最長的兩根飛羽。尾長一般，或偏短，尾羽長度依序遞變。跗蹠比中趾長上許多，覆蓋著不明顯的鱗片；腳趾修長，外趾比內趾短，並匯集在中趾根部；後趾修長，並有一只長而彎曲的爪子。
這些鳥習性活躍，經常飛行，會在高空邊飛邊發出牠們獨特的鳴叫聲，與鷦鶯屬（Prinia）（Horsf.）和Calamanthus屬（Gould）4[2]皆不同。這兩個我在福爾摩沙採集到的獨特物種，我將牠們分別命名為棕扇尾鶯（Tinnabulans，目前學名Cisticola juncidis）和黃頭扇尾鶯（Volitans，目前學名Cisticola exilis）。前者在福爾摩沙西北部的平原和丘陵隨處可見，我後來發現在上海長滿燈心草的土丘（tumuli）上也棲息著許多棕扇尾鶯。後者常見於福爾摩沙的東部和東北部。現在我想冒昧跟大家談談這兩個鳥種大致的形態特徵及我所觀察到的習性。
棕扇尾鶯（Calamanthella tinnabulans）：鳥喙嘴峰長1/3（參考原稿標示法）英吋；鳥喙至嘴裂長7/12英吋；跗蹠長2/3英吋；爪長1/2英吋；中趾長7/12英吋；翼長1 11/12英吋；尾長17/12英吋。全長4 1/2英吋。

---

[1] 審定註：本篇開頭的DENTIROSTRES、LUSCINIDAE、Calamanthella，這三者是已不再使用的歷史學名，不建議硬翻，原因有二：一來是硬翻是創新詞，但幾乎不會用到；二來是譯詞無法有查閱文獻功能，因此建議保留原文。
[2] 審定註：學名的命名者，是學名的附屬資訊，不用翻譯，若強行翻譯反而無法成為查資料的關鍵字。

##--SPLIT--##
鳥喙呈黃色，尖端和上喙上緣呈木棕色。雙腳為赭黃色。虹膜為橘黃色。上半身呈土黃色，而非赭紅色；頭部、背部、覆羽（wing-coverts）、次級飛羽（secondaries）和尾上覆羽（tail-coverts）為深棕色，帶土黃色邊緣。翅膀的羽毛為髮棕色，邊緣寬，呈淺土黃色。尾部由十二根飛羽組成，長度幾乎是平均依序遞變；尾羽為棕色，邊緣顏色較淺；其他十根尾羽呈深棕色和赭紅色，每根羽毛頂端都為純白色。過眼線（eye-streak）、喉部和下半身為摻雜著赭色或淡黃色的白色，兩側顏色較深，脇部（flanks）接近紅色。
習性：香山和福爾摩沙西北岸有許多這種生氣勃勃的小鳥，牠們棲息在長草叢和燈心草間，會豎起尾巴從一片葉子跳到另一片上，牠們的體型又靈巧又輕盈，經常被人誤認為蝗蟲，因為牠們的棲地附近也有大量的蝗蟲。牠門會突然騰空而起，飛升到稍高的地方，發出「踢─踢─踢─」的鳥鳴，然後再「叮─叮─叮─」地一邊鳴叫一邊降落；當許多小鳥同時在平原上歌唱時，其效果可比作「催眠的叮噹鈴響」，「讓遠方的羊群安然入睡」[3]。
在上海時我曾寫下以下紀錄：「這裡也有福爾摩沙叮叮鳥（Formosan Tinkler），牠在空中飛行時依然唱著牠那奇特的音符；牠正朝下呈曲線俯衝，宛如就要落地，卻再度迅速飛起，在空中盤旋，過程中鳥鳴從未停息。最後牠總算決定降落，飛落在玉米或其他植物的莖稈上，小心翼翼地用嘴喙整理羽翼，然後稍做休息；幾分鐘後，牠又再次展翅飛翔。」
福爾摩沙的中國人墾民稱這種鳥為「望冬(Mangtang)」[4]；在廈門，人們用同樣的名字來稱呼縫葉鶯（學名為Orthotomus sp.）和多數同科的小型鳥。
黃頭扇尾鶯（Calamanthella volitans）：嘴峰長超過1/3英吋；喙至嘴裂1/2英吋；跗蹠長2/3英吋；爪長5/12英吋；中趾長1/3英吋；翼長12/3英吋；尾長11/12英吋。全長3 1/4英吋。
鳥喙為淺木棕色。雙腳為稻草色。虹膜為橘黃色。頭部、後頸（nape）和整個下半身為略帶紅褐色的淺赭黃色。背部和翅膀為髮棕色，羽毛邊緣呈淺土黃色。腰部為淺赭棕色。尾部為深髮棕色；尾羽長度依序漸變，末端較寬，使尾巴看起來像一等腰三角形。

[3] 譯註：「催眠的叮噹鈴響讓遠方的羊群安然入睡」出自英國詩人托馬斯・格雷（Thomas Gray, 1716-1771）的詩作〈作於鄉村教堂墓園的輓歌〉（"Elegy Written in a Country Churchyard"）。
[4] 審定註：這是鷦鶯和扇尾鶯鳥類的台語通稱，芒冬稻仔、晚冬稻仔、暗冬稻仔都有人寫。參考：https://taigi-pahkho.wikia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%9B%E5%86%AC%E4%B3%82%E4%BB%94。

習性：這個鳥種的體型比前述的棕扇尾鶯更小，大量棲息在福爾摩沙東北部長滿草的丘陵上。牠飛行的高度遠遠超過牠的同種近親，而且能長距離飛行。飛行時牠會發出類似「唧─唧─啾─啾─」的鳥鳴；當牠消失在遼闊的天空中，你看不到鳥兒蹤影，但當牠的叫聲一傳入你耳裡，你會意識到牠就在你頭頂上的某處。

屬：鷦鶯屬（霍斯菲德創立）。
斑紋鷦鶯（Prinia striata）（新種）：嘴峰長4/10英吋；喙至喙裂6/10英吋；跗蹠長9/10英吋；翼長2 1/3英吋；尾長4 7/10英吋。全長8英吋。
鳥喙為黑色。雙腳為赭黃色。虹膜為橘棕色。上半身為飽滿的棕色，每根羽毛中心都較深，邊緣略帶白色，外觀呈條紋狀。翅膀為略黃的髮棕色；羽毛邊緣寬，呈土黃色。尾部為淺棕色；羽軸顏色深，頂端略帶黃色。下半身呈暗黃色調，兩頰和頭部有班點，頸部側邊有條紋。腿部為紅棕色。
這隻鳥是在香山西北側，離內陸不遠的地方捕獲的。牠比任何我在中國見過的鷦鶯都要來得大。在牠慣常活動的地區，可以看到牠棲息在某些高草莖的頂端，重複唱著由幾個音符組成的單調小曲，叫聲聽起來與鷚鵐（Bunting Lark）有些相像。

科：鶇科（Turdidae）。亞科：蟻鶇亞科（FORMICARINAE）。屬：HYDROBATA（Vieill創立）或稱河烏。
在世界各地不同的區域似乎存在幾種或多或少有些相似的河烏。諸如美洲的北美河烏（H. unicolor (Pr. Buon.)，目前學名 Cinclus mexicanus）、歐洲的白喉河（H. Pallisir (Temm.)，目前學名Cinclus cinclus）和河烏（H. Asiatica (Swains)，目前學名Cinclus pallasi）。我傾向於將此處提及的這種鳥歸類為最後一個鳥種，但對此我仍然有些疑慮，因此我建議我們先用福爾摩沙河烏（Hydrobata marila，目前學名Cinclus pallasi）這個屬名來描述牠：福爾摩沙河烏全長8 1/2英吋；翼長（始於翅膀基部彎曲處）為3 3/4英吋。
其鳥喙和雙腳為靛藍色；腳底厚實粗糙，帶有強壯的腳爪。虹膜為深棕色。全身呈赭棕色，下半身顏色較深，上半身帶著一點生赭色。一條白線幾乎包圍了眼睛，翅膀的覆羽上有些模糊的白色斑點。

##--SPLIT--##
以上描述的標本是這一隻雌鳥，是我們在前往內陸探勘硫磺礦坑的途中，在某座瀑布上射殺的。我們看見牠往水面俯衝，然後豎起尾巴，停坐在潺潺小溪中的一塊石頭上。

第三亞科：畫眉亞科（TIMALINAE）或稱畫眉（BABBLERS）。屬：噪鶥屬（GARRULAX，Leal創立）。
畫眉（Garrulax Taewanus） （新種）：畫眉（Song-thrush or Hwa-mei，學名為G. Sinensis，目前學名 Garrulax canorus）在福州附近的丘陵隨處可見，有著醒目的白色過眼線，在福爾摩沙丘陵地區取而代之出現的則是另一種顏色較黯淡的物種，缺乏眉部的特徵，體型描述如下──雄鳥：全長9 3/4英吋；翼長3 3/4英吋；尾長4 1/4英吋。雌鳥：全長9英吋；翼長3 7/12英吋；尾長3 3/4英吋。兩者的嘴峰長皆為8/10英吋；喙至嘴裂9/10英吋；跗蹠長1 9/10英吋。
鳥喙為銅黃色。雙腿為赭色。虹膜為灰色。頭頂和下半身為淺赭色；頭部羽毛以及一些頸部和胸部兩側的羽毛帶有黑色條紋。腹部為煙灰色。上半身為暗橄欖棕色，兩翼顏色較為明亮，尾羽表面隱約帶有一些橫紋。
這種鳥的歌聲非常優美，但就像所有同屬的畫眉，牠極少會鳴唱到數分鐘那麼久。在山上散步時，附近的樹叢中突然傳出美妙的旋律，令我驚艷不已，當我走近，我看見那隻鳥無聲無息地飛進了身旁的隱蔽處中，那裡還有幾隻牠的同伴，直到我走遠，牠們才繼續引吭啁啾。雄鳥和雌鳥的外觀非常相像。

屬：鉤嘴鶥屬（POMATORRHINUS，霍斯菲德創立）
這個屬被評論為一種不歌唱的畫眉，但在福爾摩沙隨處可見的這種鳥種，是以美妙的歌聲聞名，此外，牠也和在福州附近北嶺山上採集到的那種類似小型鷦鷯（Wren）的物種大不相同。
小彎嘴（Pomatorrhinus musicus，目前學名Pomatorhinus musicus）（新種）：雄鳥全長7 4/10英吋，雌全總長7 8/10英吋。翼長3 1/3英吋；尾長3 1/2英吋；嘴峰長8/10英吋；喙至嘴裂1英吋；跗蹠長1 1/3英吋；爪9/10英吋；中趾長1 1/10英吋。
上喙為黑色，下喙略帶黃色。雙腳為深靛藍。頭頂和兩頰為墨綠色。

##--SPLIT--##
嘴喙和眼睛之間為深黑色。眼睛上有一道條紋，喉部和胸部為白色，胸部帶有黑色斑點。後頸和腹部上部為美麗的栗紅色。其餘的羽毛為橄欖綠色；雄鳥的羽毛則摻雜著濃栗色，翅膀上尤其明顯。
這些畫眉的鳴唱讓長滿矮林的山丘生氣盎然。我們可以在某些比周圍樹叢高出一截的樹枝上，看見牠們圓圓的背影跳躍在細枝間。牠們受驚時會以驚人的速度跳到下方的灌木叢中，並在那裡低聲地啁啾不休，彷彿是在告訴同伴牠們方才所見，以及是如何死裡逃生。雄鳥喜愛在這些隱身處鳴唱，讓悅耳的旋律源源不絕地傳入過路行人的耳裡，越靠近，鳥兒們就鳴叫得越大聲，直到牠們認為陌生人已經太過靠近，構成侵擾，才會悄悄溜出樹叢到另個地點，而當你焦急等待為了看牠一眼，又會聽見牠跑到遠處繼續發出美妙的樂音。

第三目：SCANSORKS。第四科：杜鵑科（CUCULIDAE）。第三亞科：美洲鵑亞科（COCCYZINAE）（地鵑（Ground Cuckoos））屬：鴉鵑屬（CENTROPUS，Illig.）。
番鵑（Centropus dimidiatus，目前學名 Centropus bengalensis）：（或是Blyth所定名）全長12 3/4英吋；翼長5 9/10英吋；尾長7英吋；嘴峰長1英吋；喙至嘴裂1 1/10英吋；跗蹠長1 4/10英吋。
頭部及頸部的羽毛和覆羽皆帶有堅硬的尖羽軸。鳥喙和雙腳為黑色。虹膜為血紅色。頭部、頸部、下半身和尾巴均呈綠黑色。背部和雙翼為淺栗棕色；多數羽毛的羽軸為黃白色，尤其是覆羽。羽軸基（quill）越接近頂端越偏棕色。腰部的羽毛十分柔軟，夾雜著棕色和暗黃色的橫條紋。
我第一次在福爾摩沙東北岸的蘇澳（Saw-o）和基隆那邊的山上見到這種鳥時，當時我以為牠和福州山谷裡隨處可見的那種鳥（屬於外來留鳥的褐翅鴉鵑（Centropus Philippensis Cuv. or Crow Pheasant，目前學名Centropus sinensis））是同一種物種，牠們以「呼─呼─呼─」的鳴叫聲聞名，這種叫聲常被比作風琴音栓被突然拉出時所產生的聲響。可是採集到一隻雄鳥後（前述的特徵描述便是基於這隻雄鳥的標本）

##--SPLIT--##
發現牠的體型幾乎比另個物種大上超過一半，顏色也有所不同。除此之外，牠的鳴叫聲也不一樣，在「呼─呼─呼─」後會重複三次雙音節的「咔答」聲，牠發出的叫聲有點類似於那種樵夫斧頭敲擊在遠方森林中某棵老樹樹幹上的聲響。抬頭往山坡上看，你便能經常看見這種的小雲雀，醒目地坐在樹叢頂端，頭和脖子邊向前彎曲邊發出奇特的鳥鳴。

我或許可以繼續補充另一個鳥種，雖然牠並不是新發現的物種，但我在中國沿岸還不曾見過。那就是菲律賓玄燕鷗（Philippine Noddy）。
第八目：雁鴨目（ANSERES）。第五科：鷗科（LARIDAE）。第三亞科：燕鷗亞科（STERNIDAE）（燕鷗（Terns））。屬：玄燕鷗屬（ANÖUS，Leach創立）
玄燕鷗（Anöus pileatus，Scop.），即菲律賓玄燕鷗。雄鳥全長14 1/2英吋；翼長10 8/10英吋；尾長6 1/5英吋。雌鳥全長14 1/2英吋；翼長10 8/10英吋；尾長6 3/10英吋；鳥喙上緣1 1/2英吋；喙至喙裂2 1/5英吋；膝上無羽毛覆蓋的脛骨（tibia）約 1/2英吋；跗蹠長9/10英吋；中趾長1 6/10英吋；爪長4/10英吋。
鳥喙和雙腳為黑色。虹膜為深棕色。前額和頭頂為白色，延伸至枕部（occiput）時漸變為煙灰色，接著加深為棕色。嘴喙和眼睛之間為深黑色。眼周有一圈白色。其餘的羽毛為棕黑色，羽軸基顏色更深。
在蘇澳灣，有隻菲律賓玄燕鷗的雄鳥飛得極為靠近大船的輕艇，於是一名船員用手將牠打落，而在基隆，有些漁夫活捉了一隻雌鳥，向我們兜售。在離岸已遠的海上，我經常看見這些玄燕鷗遠遠跟在船後，成群來回疾飛，穿梭於汽船快速前進時在水面上留下的寬大尾跡上，我猜測牠們是在掠食小魚，這一大群小魚因海水劇烈波動而驚慌失措，對身處的自然狀態感到十分疑惑，有好一陣子將自己暴露在危險的空氣之中。史溫侯書寫史料翻譯。
