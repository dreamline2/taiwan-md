## Description

史溫侯補充廈門大部分的鳥類，都可以在島上找到。事實上，我在廈門收到的標本，其中大多數都來自福爾摩沙。

XLV〈廈門鳥類學〉之進一步修訂與增補，以及一些關於《福爾摩沙鳥類》的評論
撰寫者 史溫侯副領事\* \*史溫侯先生與編輯之間的信件
當我細讀我在《英國鳥類科學期刊》（The Ibis）第五期的文章〈廈門鳥類學〉（Ornithology of Amoy）時，發現有些地方需要更正，我趕緊寫信給你，好儘快把這些錯誤公之於眾。
遠東樹鶯[1]（Arundinax canturians, sp. 32，目前學名 Horornis canturians）以及A. miniatus（不是minutus）, sp. 33應該跟日本樹鶯（Salicaria cantans，目前學名 Horornis diphone）以及《日本動物誌》（Fauna Japonica）裡的S. cantillans再做一次比較[2]，以免牠們被視為有效物種（good species）。正確來說，我認為牠們應該在蝗鶯屬（Lusciniopsis目前屬名Locustella）之下。
白眉黃鶲雌鳥（Thamnobia niceiventris, mihi, sp. 44），完全不是鵲鴝屬（Thamnobia，目前屬名 Copsychus）。我不懂為何我會犯下這麼大的錯誤。這裡描述的是一隻雌鳥，布萊思先生（Mr. Blyth）認為牠應該跟孟加拉的白尾鴝（Erythrosterna leucura （Gmel.），目前學名Myiomela leucura）有關。我們採集到的標本全都是雌鳥以及幼鳥，直到最近施萊格爾[3]（M. Schlegel）先生才射到一隻雄鳥，其下半身的紅色部分，證明這個物種應該是《日本動物誌》裡的白眉黃鶲（Erythrosterna mugimaki，目前學名Ficedula mugimaki），而不是印度的鳥。
白鶺鴒黑背眼紋亞種（Motacilla lugens （v. lugubris），目前學名Motacilla alba），在《日本動物誌》裡中也有提到過這種鳥，這只標本是在暴風雨過後在這裡射殺的。牠應該被插入在呂宋白鶺鴒[4]（M. luzoniensis，目前學名Motacilla alba）之後[5]。

Sp. 57。這裡提到一個名字Turdus advena，所指的應該是白腹鶇（Turdus daulias，現被認為是白腹鶇（Turdus pallidus）之同物異名）才對。
說到之前提過的鶇（Thrush），我已經成功找到《日本動物誌》裡提到的烏灰鶇（Turdus （Merula） cardis，目前學名Turdus cardis），這種鳥在這裡的數量很多。牠有類似於鶇和黑鶇（blackbird）的羽毛，羽色介於兩者之間，牠具體展現了鶇屬（Turdus）的這兩個分支之間有相當緊密的關聯。
Sp. 61，Garrulax rugillatus, nobis，毫無疑問應該是黑臉噪鶥（Garrulax perspicillatus （Gmelin））。
我們曾經在這個島上射到過中國畫眉（G. canorus （Latham）），即「中國歌鶇」（Chinese Song Thrush），或稱畫眉（Hwa-mei （" pictured eyebrow ''））。
Sp. 64[6]，黑喉紅臀鵯（Pycnonotus hemorrhous，目前學名 Pycnonotus cafer ），是「中國的黑頭食蠅者（Le Gobe-mouche à tête noire）」；歐洲斑姬鶲（Muscicapa atricapilla, Vieillot（此為歐洲斑姬鶲之學名，但本種並不分布於東亞及南亞），目前學名Ficedula hypoleuca）；但不巧的是，同屬中還有另一種來自錫蘭的鳥；黑翅雀鵯（Ægithina atricapilla, Vieillot，目前學名 Aegithina tiphia ），也具有相同的學名，現在的問題是：哪個優先？[7]
Sp. 87，黑頭文鳥（Munia molucca，目前學名Lonchura atricapilla。我從《Shaw's Zoology》發現，此物種是Munia minima （Lath.），M. molucca是黑頭文鳥（M. rubro-nigra ）的同種異名。
Sp. 106，小鵪鶉（Coturnix chinensis，目前學名Coturnix japonica），應為普通鵪鶉[8]（Coturnix dactylisonans，目前學名Coturnix coturnix）。這附近並沒有發現小鵪鶉。

Sp. 120。棕夜鷺（Nycticorax manillensis，目前學名Nycticorax caledonicus）應該是夜鷺（N. griseus，目前學名Nycticorax nycticorax）才對。不久前，我在香港的一個私人標本儲藏室看過棕夜鷺（N. manillensis）的標本，那是一個完全不一樣的物種。

雖然我只看過幾眼福爾摩沙的鳥類，但毫無疑問，廈門大部分的鳥類，都可以在島上找到。事實上，我在廈門收到的標本，其中大多數都來自福爾摩沙；上次我搭乘「不屈號」旅行時，也設法採集了幾種物種，對我來說，他們算是新的物種。我在上海的《皇家亞洲學會華北分會期刊》（Journal of the N. C. B. of the Asiatic Society）[12]中對此也有相關的描述。
學報中描述的第一隻鳥，棕扇尾鶯（Calamanthella tintinnabulans，目前學名Cisticola juncidis），毫無疑問是《日本動物誌》裡的棕扇尾鶯（Cisticola brunneiceps，目前學名Cisticola juncidis），而C. brunneiceps一樣有可能只是該物種在歐洲的地區變異族群。
黃頭扇尾鶯臺灣亞種（C. volitans，目前學名Cisticola exilis ）是一個分類歸屬明確的物種（good species）。
斑紋鷦鶯（Prinia striata，目前學名Prinia crinigera），我現在比較傾向稱牠為一種Drymoica。牠的身型比一般的鷦鶯屬（Prinia）還長，而且有一條引人注目的長尾巴。
河烏（Dipper）很可能是Cinclus pallasii[13]，因為我上次看到有人引述，說牠出現在日本。
臺灣畫眉（Garrulax taewanus）和小彎嘴（Pomatorhinus musicus）皆為分類歸屬明確的物種。
番鵑（Centropus dimidiatus （?），目前學名Centropus bengalensis）（林中居民（the Woodman））。我曾經在廈門和香港發現過，在廈門牠是迷鳥，在香港則是留鳥。我會寄標本給你檢查。
玄燕鷗指名亞種（Anoiis pileatus[14]，目前學名Anous stolidus）需要與玄燕鷗（A. stolidus）做比較。
我在〈福爾摩沙島紀行〉（Narrative of a Visit to Formosa）中，寫錯了一些鳥名，我還在你的備註（前文，頁89）裡看到，請容我訂正

，Alauda minuta和Dicrurus malabaricus應該是A. cælivox（小雲雀華南亞種，目前學名Alauda gulgula coelivox）和D. macrocercus（大卷尾）才對。
在中國，除了四川（Se-tehuen）和廣西（Quangsi）之外，是沒有鸚鵡（Parrot）的，所以我不相信福爾摩沙會有鸚鵡。事實上，海南島（the island of Hainan）也沒有鸚鵡，而當地出產的九官鳥屬（Gracula）可能是最像爪哇的鸚鵡的物種了。兀鷲（Vulture）也是，有人說兀鷲是來自廣東，但一定是有人從非常南邊的某個地方帶來廣東的。這座城市附近不可能會有兀鷲。[15]

XLV Further Corrections and Additions to the “Ornithology of Amoy,” with some Remarks on the Birds of Formosa. By Robert Swinhoe, of H. M. Consular Service\*.
A few necessary corrections having again suggested themselves on reperusing my article on the “Ornithology of Amoy,” as printed in the 5th Number of ‘The Ibis,’ I hasten to communicate them to you, that they may appear before the public as soon as possible.
Arundinax canturians, sp. 32, and A. miniatus (nee minutus), sp. 33, should be again compared with Salicaria cantans and S. cantillans of the ‘Fauna Japonica’ before they can be considered as good species. They belong more correctly, I think, to the genus Lusciniopsis.
Thamnobia niveiventris, mihi, sp. 44, is no Thamnobia at all. I cannot understand how I came to be so mistaken. The description given is that of a female bird, which Mr. Blyth considers to be referable to Erythrosterna leucura (Gmel.) of Bengal. All the specimens we procured were females and immature birds until lately, when M. Schlegel shot a male. The extent of red on the lower parts of this individual proves the species to the Erythrosterna mugimaki of the ‘Fauna Japonica’ rather than the Indian bird.
Motacilla lugens (v. lugubris), mentioned in the ‘Fauna Japonica,’ has also been shot here after stormy weather, and should be inserted after M. luzoniensis.

- Communicated in a letter from Mr. Swinhoe to the Editor.

Sp. 57. For ‘Turdus advena,’ mentioned here, read ‘Turdus daulias,’ to which species the name refers.
Of the other Thrushes alluded to, I have succeeded in identifying Turdus (Merula) cardis of the ‘Fauna Japonica.’ This bird occurs here abundantly, in both its thrush-like and blackbird-like plumages, with the intermediate grades, and presents a happy link between the two subdivisions of the genus Turdus.
Sp. 61, Garrulax rugillatus, nobis, is undoubtedly Garrulax perspicillatus (Gmelin).
We have since shot in the island G. canorus (Latham), the “Chinese Song Thrush,” or Hwa-mei (“pictured eyebrow”).
Sp. 64, Pycnonotus hæmorrhous, is “Le Gobe-mouche à tête noire de la Chine” of Sonnerat; Muscicapa atricapilla, Vieillot; but, unfortunately, there is another bird of the same genus (the Ægithina atricapilla, Vieillot) from Ceylon bearing the same specific name: which has the priority?\*
Sp. 87, Munia molucca. I find, from ‘Shaw’s Zoology,’ that this species is Munia minima (Lath.), M. molucca being synonymous with M. rubro-nigra.
Sp. 106, Coturnix chinensis. This is Coturnix dactylisonans. C. chinensis is not found in this neighbourhood.
Sp. 115. For Herodias asha substitute Butorides javanicus (Horsf.), of which species several examples have been met with lately.
Sp. 120, Nycticorax manillensis, should be probably N. griseus. N. manillensis, of which I saw an example the other day in a private cabinet at Hong Kong, is a very different species.

The immense forests of Camphor-trees that cover the gigantic snow-capped mountains are no doubt productive of numerous species known to science; but until the right of travelling is established in China, exploration of such wild fastnesses will be impossible; and even were Chinese Formosa open to Europeans, an attempt to ascent these lovely heights would always be attended with danger, owing to the extreme savageness of the aboriginal tribes. With the exception of the S.W. portion, where birds are abundant, especially Orioles and Drongos, the coast, though green and well-wooded, is comparatively destitute of feathered denizens.
I have no doubt, from the glance I had at the birds of Formosa, that most of our Amoy forms are found in the island. Indeed, I have received examples of most of them from there. The few species that I procured in our last trip in the ‘Inflexible’ which were new to me I described at Shanghai, in the Journal of the N. C. B. of the Asiatic Society\*.
The first bird therein described, Calamanthella tintinnabulans, is, without doubt, the Cisticola brunneiceps of the ‘Fauna Japonica,’ which again is perhaps only a local variety of the European species.
C. volitans is a good species.
Prinia striata I should be more inclined now, I think, to call a Drymoica. It is much longer than the ordinary Priniæ, and has a remarkably long tail.
The Dipper is most likely Cinclus pallasii, as I see this last quoted as occurring in Japan.
Garrulax taewanus and Pomatorhinus musicus are both good species.
Centropus dimidiatus (?) (“the Woodman”) I have since found as a straggler at Amoy, and as a resident at Hong Kong. I am sending you specimens for examination.
Anoiis pileatus requires comparison with A. stolidus.
In my “Narrative of a Visit to Formosa,” a few wrong names of birds had crept in, and I see that they have been inserted in your remarks (anteà, p. 89). Allow me therefore to correct

Alauda minuta and Dicrurus malabaricus, which ought to stand A. cælivox and D. macrocercus.

  I cannot believe in the existence of a Parrot in Formosa. None occur in China, unless in the provinces of Se-tchuen and Quangsi. Indeed, the island of Hainan, which produces a Gracula, most probably identical with the Javanese species, is not known to contain a Parrot. The Vulture too, which has been said to be from Canton, must have been brought there from the extreme south somewhere. Certainly in the neighbourhood of that city none occur\*.
