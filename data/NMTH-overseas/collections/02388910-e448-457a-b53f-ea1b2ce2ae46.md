## Description

史溫侯紀錄之前在廈門、澳門等地鳥類觀察。

## 中文 (TRANSLATION)

##--SPLIT--##
史溫侯先生的上一封信，地點為福爾摩沙淡水，日期為去年的1月17日，信上寫說：
「我目前在鳥類這方面沒什麼進展，因為我們還沒在這個港口安頓下來，不過下個月，我也許會有新發現。
斑紋鷦鶯（Suya striata, nobis，目前學名 Prinia crinigera）在這裡的丘陵地很常見，而且會讓我想到黃頭扇尾鶯臺灣亞種（Cisticola volitans，目前學名 Cisitcola exilis），但我在這趟旅程中尚未見過後者；

##--SPLIT--##
至於那些令人驚嘆的鸚鵡（Parrot），也還沒有消息。我應該跟你說過，前年夏天有人在廣東射到三隻長尾鸚鵡（Parrakeet），但我認為牠們充其量只能算是偶然來到那附近的訪客。我猜想，跟城裡販賣的吸蜜鸚鵡（Lories）、連雀1（Wax-wings）、孔雀（Peacock）或青鸞（Argus Pheasants）一樣，短尾鸚鵡（Loriculus puniculus，目前學名Loriculus vernalis）同樣沒資格宣稱廣東是牠的棲息地。如果我在香港或廣東有代理人，能幫忙我留意賣鳥的商店，我應該可以好好地蒐集到一批所謂的中國鳥再寄給你。我不知道這些鳥是怎麼流通到特定地區的，除非我從真的有在野外遇到牠們，而且有證據證明牠們不是被放出來的籠養鳥。日本鹿（Japanese Deer）和某些其他的鹿科（Cervidae）可見於香港的丘陵地，但如果我們認為牠們是原生物種，那就大錯特錯了。因為如果我們花點時間詢問，就會知道是這一些愛好運動的歐洲人把好幾種類似的動物帶到殖民地野放的。
當我經過廈門時，我初次遇見一隻《日本動物誌》（Fauna Japonica）裡的白眉鶲（Muscicapa hylocharis，目前學名Ficedula zanthopygia）。牠很漂亮。也許你有興趣刊出關於這個物種的第一手描述，我將我日誌中的筆記抄寫給你：
白眉鶲（Muscicapa hylocharis, Schlegel，目前學名Ficedula zanthopygia），♂，廈門，1861年11月27日。第一眼看起來像雄性的紅胸鶲（Erythrosterna parva，目前學名Ficedula parva），但尾巴沒有白色色塊。體長5.1英吋；翼長2.85英吋；尾長2.2英吋。嘴喙丁香棕色帶點黑；腿和爪同上，腳底顏色較淡。嘴內側肉色帶點黃，舌頭邊緣帶點黑色。耳朵偏圓形，大小等同眼睛，嘴喙筆直，嘴喙隆起最末端有新月形蓋板。喉部和胸部的顏色跟歐亞鴝相比多了點黃色，而腋窩和脅部的顏色較淺，偏金黃色；眼睛上緣和下緣的顏色較淺，且摻雜了橄欖綠。腹部和下腹部是白色。上半部為橄欖綠帶點棕色。翅膀和尾部為髮棕色，前者的羽毛邊緣呈現赭色帶點黃，這特徵在次級飛羽、小覆羽和次級覆羽上更為明顯。
解剖──食道（oesophagus）約0.1英吋寬，

在前胃（proventriculus）處擴大為0.25英吋。胃略呈圓形，堅硬且多肌肉，不是很緊貼，側邊有小肌腱；深度與寬度皆為0.4英吋；長度0.6英吋。上皮組織（epithelium）為土黃色，多皺紋，薄且類似皮革，內有甲蟲殘骸以及某種莓果的琥珀黃橢圓形種子。腸長度6.7英吋，厚度0.1至0.5英吋，沒有發現盲腸。心臟0.4英吋長，0.2英吋寬。
我一直在翻閱培里（Perry2）的《日本遠征》（Expedition to Japan）第二冊，裡頭列舉了在中國採集到的鳥類，大部分都有海涅先生（Mr. Heine3）簡短的註解。這位先生是那次遠征的隨行畫家。我曾有幸與他會面，他宣稱熱愛鳥類（feathered tribes），我原本以為對於那些有機會在牠們天然棲息地看到的種類，他應該會有更詳盡的評論。如果書中這些鳥類插圖被視為美國人所能做出的最好作品，我必須要說它們遠遠落後這個寫實圖像的時代。如果允許我提出批評（以我對中國鳥類的了解，由我來提出一些意見應該也還說得過去），我想冒昧發表一些評論。

    黑臉噪鶥（Garrulax perspicillatus），插畫4。插圖中的鳥，就這種其身體比例來說，嘴喙跟頭部太小，而插圖中鳥的尺寸比牠的實際大小要小了許多。現實中這種鳥眼睛為淡褐色。
    灰椋鳥（Sturnus cinereus，目前學名Spodiopsar cineraceus ），插圖5。圖中鳥的頭部應該要再扁平一點，而且虹膜是白的。
    關於澳門鳥類稀少的原因，海涅先生提出的解釋更是大有問題。在澳門對鳥類趕盡殺絕得並不是中國人，而是混血的葡萄牙人或澳門人，他們每個禮拜天都大舉出擊，所有男人和男孩帶著槍枝，無情地殺死他們舉目所及的每一隻小鳥。在賈梅士公園（Camoens’ garden4），因為不允許人們帶槍進入，鳥類隨處可見，而且比較溫馴。
    灰燕鵙（Artamus fuscus）。我認為這個鳥種一定是被標記錯了。我非常懷疑牠是否真的是在澳門被發現。
    黑枕藍鶲（Hypothymis cyanomelana，目前學名Hypothymis azurea）。說這種鳥「棲息在灌木叢中」，是指什麼意思？如果是指牠偶爾飛落在灌木頂，或停在某些外延的枝幹上，那十分正確。但這種鳥，習性上完全就像鶲（Flycatcher）一樣，

從來沒想過牠會在灌木周圍活動，或是像鶯類（Warblers）般在灌叢裡活動。而這個評論或許同樣也適用於下一個鳥種，藍尾鴝（Lusciola cyanura，目前學名Tarsiger cyanurus ）。
藍磯鶇（Petrocincla manilensis，目前學名Monticola solitarius）。我曾多次採集到這種鳥的標本，下半部沒有一絲鐵鏽色，而且看起來跟布萊思先生（Mr. Blyth）給我的藍腹藍磯鶇（P. pandoo，目前學名Monticola solitarius pandoo）樣本幾乎沒有兩樣。我不得不同意這位先生的看法，他認為這兩種鳥都是藍磯鶇（P. cyanea，目前學名Monticola solitarius），腹部和下腹部的紅色可能是因為特定氣候因素造成的，就像中杜鵑（Cuculus tenuirostris，目前學名Cuculus saturates）有不同外形變異一樣。
白喉紅臀鵯（Ixos hamorrhous，目前學名Pycnonotus aurigaster），插圖6。插圖中的冠羽完全錯誤。這種鳥沒有羽冠，牠的冠狀黑色羽毛只是普通長度。
紅冠水雞（Gallinula chloropus）。我在廈門看過這種鳥，後來我在福爾摩沙有弄到一對。
太平洋金斑鴴（Charadrius pluvialis，目前學名Pluvialis fulva）。這一定是鑑定錯誤。這種中國的鳥類，我見過一大群，毫無疑問地可以將牠們歸屬為金斑鴴（C. virginicus，目前學名Pluvialis fulva）。
小燕鷗（Sterna minuta，目前學名Sternula albifrons）。這種鳥偶爾會在中國沿海出沒，但是我至今在福爾摩沙見過的所有小型燕鷗（Tern）都屬於其他鳥種──也許是眼斑白額燕鷗（S. nereis of Gould，目前學名Sternula nereis）。我們可以在琉球（Loochoo）發現福爾摩沙的鳥族群。

## 英文 (ORIGINAL)

##--SPLIT--##
Mr. Swinhoe’s last letter, dated Tamsuy in Formosa, January 17th last, says :—
“I am not doing much in birds just now, as we are scarcely settled yet at this new port; but by next month you may hear of my making some progress.
“Suya striata, nobis, is common on these hills, and, as I imagine, Cisticola volitans; but the latter I have not seen this trip. No

##--SPLIT--##
news of the wonderful Parrot yet. I think I told you of the three Parrakeets shot at Canton the summer before last, but, at the best, they can only be looked upon as occasional visitants to that neighbourhood. I suspect Loriculus puniculus has no more claim to Canton as a habitat than have the Lories, the Wax-wings, the Peacocks, or the Argus Pheasants that are offered for sale in that city. I could send a fine collection of so-called Chinese birds, if I only had an agent at Hong Kong or Canton to watch the bird-shops; but I never heed the assignation of birds to particular localities, unless I have the authority of some person that has actually met with them in a wild state, and has proofs that they are not caged birds let loose. The Japanese Deer and some other species of Cervidæ may be found on the hills of Hong Kong; but we should be very wrong in considering them indigenous there; for if we only take the trouble to inquire, we may learn that several animal of this kind have been set free in that colony by some sport-loving Europeans.
“In passing through Amoy, I met with, for the first time, a fine male of the Muscicapa hylocharis of the ‘Fauna Japonica’; and as you may like to publish a description of this interesting species, taken from a fresh example, I will transcribe the note from my journal :—
“Muscicapa hylocharis, Schlegel, ♂, Amoy, November 27, 1861. Looks at first sight like the male of Erythrosterna parva, but there is no white on the tail. Length 5.1; wing, 2.85; tail, 2.2 in. Bill blackish clove-brown; legs and claws ditto, with paler soles. Inside of the mouth yellowish flesh-colour, with a blackish rim to the tongue. Ear roundish, of the size of the eye, the diameter bone being nearly at right angles to the direction of the bill, and the crescent-shaped operculum placed on the arc furthest from the bill. Throat and breast yellowish robin-colour, rather golden, paler on the axillaries and flanks; pale and mixed with olive over and under the eye, the cheek being mottled with darker olive. Belly and vent white. Upper parts brownish olive-green. Wings and tail hair-brown, the former margined with yellowish ochre, more strongly on the tertiaries, small coverts and secondary coverts.
“Dissection.—CEsophagus about 1 inch wide, enlarging at

##--SPLIT--##
proventriculus to .25. Stomach roundish, hard and muscular, not much adpressed, with small lateral tendons; depth and breadth, .4 in.; length, .6. Epithelium sienna-coloured, rugose, thin and leathery, containing remains of beetles and amber-yellow oval seeds of some berry. Intestine 6-8 in. long, .1 to .15 thick, no cæca discoverable. Heart .4 in. long, .2 broad.
“I have been looking through the second volume of Perry’s ‘Expedition to Japan,’ wherein the birds collected in China are enumerated, with short notes on most of them by Mr. Heine. This gentleman, who was artist to the Expedition, I had the pleasure of meeting, and from the love he professed for the feathered tribes, I should have expected fuller remarks on the species he had had the opportunity of seeing in their native haunts. If the few plates of birds in this volume can be looked upon as the best the Americana can produce, I must say they are far behind this age of truthful iconography. If I may be allowed to criticise (and my acquaintance with Chinese birds may justify me, in so far as they are concerned), I will venture on a few remarks.
“Garrulax perspicillatus, plate 4. The bill and head of this figure are by far too small in proportion to the size of the bird given, which is a good deal smaller than life-size. The eye in the living bird is hazel.
“Sturnus cinereus, plate 5. The head of this figure should be flatter, and the iris white.
“Mr. Heine’s reason for the scarcity of birds at Macao is rather erroneous. It is not the Chinese who exterminate the birds at that settlement, but the half-caste Portuguese or Macaese, who sally out in numbers every Sunday, each man and boy armed with a gyn, and ruthlessly destroy every small bird they can set eyes on. In Camoens’ garden, where guns are not admitted, birds are common enough, and comparatively tame.
“Artamus fuscus. I think this species must have been labelled wrong. I question much wether it is found about Macao.
“Hypothymis cyanomelæna. What is meant by this species “inhabiting bushes”? If it refers to its occasionally alighting on the tops of bushes, or settling on some exposed branch, it is correct enough. But the species is a pure Flycatcher in habits, and never

##--SPLIT--##
dreams of creeping about bushes, or even entering them after the manner of Warblers. The remark may well be applied, however, to the next species that follows, Lusciola cyanura.
“Petrocincla manilensis. I have repeatedly shot specimens of this bird without a tinge of ferruginous on the under-parts, and undistinguishable from examples of P. pandoo received from Mr. Blyth; I cannot therefore help agreeing with that gentleman, in thinking that both of the so-called species may be referred to P. cyanea, the red tints of the belly and vent being probably attributable to certain climatal causes, as is the case with the different varieties of Cuculus tenuirostris.
“Ixos hæmorrhous, plate 6. The crest given in this plate is a perfect mistake. The bird has no crest, the black crown-feathers being but of ordinary length.
“Gallinula chloropus. I once saw this bird at Amoy. I have since procured a pair at Formosa.
“Charadrius pluvialis. This must be a mistake in identification. The Chinese bird, of which I have seen a large number, is undoubtedly referable to C. virginicus.
“Sterna minuta. This bird is found occasionally on the coast of China; but all the small Terns I have yet seen in Formosa are of some other species—perhaps S. nereis of Gould. One would expect to find the Formosan species at Loochoo.”
